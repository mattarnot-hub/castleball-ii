Defense Rules Toggles
Defense carry rackets
Fielders use rackets instead of gloves — or in addition to them. Enables deflection plays and net-smash outs.
Net smash = inning over
If defense smashes ball into Gate, ALL baserunners must be touching a base or the inning ends immediately.
Catcher tag = double out
If backcatcher catches ball AND tags home simultaneously, two runners are out.
Throw-to-first out zone
Even if batter is in the safe deflection zone, a clean throw to first before they arrive = out.
Offense Rules Toggles
Offense carry rackets on base
Baserunners keep their racket around the bases. Enables deflection plays from base.
6ft Deflection Zone (outfield only)
Within 6ft of their base, a runner may deflect a teammate's hit into the outfield. Does NOT protect against defensive throws or racket hits aimed to get them out.
Foot-on-base rule (deflect)
One foot must be on the base at the moment the runner deflects. The deflection zone (6ft radius) is for outfield redirection only — the runner is still exposed to defensive plays.
Deflect into outfield only
Deflections must send the ball into the outfield — open, unoccupied territory. Cannot be used to block or interfere with a defensive play aimed at the runner.
Out Logic Toggles
Net-line cross = out at home
Ball crossing the Gate line before runner tags home = runner out. Replaces collision logic.
Caught in air = batter out
Standard catch rule applies — batter out if ball is caught before hitting ground.
